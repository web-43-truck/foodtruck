Food Truck Owner Persona

Name: John Truck

Profession

  • John is a food truck business owner that is looking for ways to optimize his time and grow his business. He enjoys creating new recipes and serving different areas in the city.

Technology

  • Iphone 15 iOS 17 with Safari as browser
  • MacBook Air with M2 Chip and 8 RAM with Safari as browser
  • Uses Apple Music to stream music from his phone and computer

Attitudes and Behaviors

  • Loves to cook.
  • Loves having a business of his own, a food truck, and the ability to control his schedule.
  • John likes having the ability to bring his business to different locations around the city.
  • He enjoys planning and coming up with ideas to grow his business.
  • He utilizes different social media platforms to provide updates on his food truck location.
  • He hates that he has to constantly update all of his social media applications with his new location.
  • John is optimistic of the future of his business.

Frustrations and Needs

  • John gets frustrated when his socials crash, and he cannot post an updated location.
  • He also gets frustrated when he has to change location mid-work day and has to change location on all socials.
  • John gets upset when he loses revenue when he forgets to update his socials on time.
  • He dislikes that he loses time updating applications.
  • He has been searching for a website or application that will make updating his location easier.

Goals

  • John would like to spend less time updating his socials.
  • John would like to update his location in a central location.

User Story

  • As a food truck owner, I want to update my food truck's location through one website.

Interaction Flow

Use Case: John needs to update his food truck's location on his socials. John is tired of changing locations on different social media platforms, but has just learned of a new website that offers the option to create a business profile and, easily, update the location of his food truck. John wants to create a new profile on the food truck locator website and post his location.

Pre-condition: John has Google's search engine open on his MacBook Air.

Post-condition: John successfully creates a food truck user profile and posts his location.

  • John searches the food truck locator website on Google and click's on the website's name.
  • The locator website opens up to the home page and displays a sign-up button.
  • John clicks on the sign-up button.
  • The website displays the sign-up form that asks for details about the profile creator and food truck business.
  • John enters his information and the information about the food truck, and he clicks submit.
  • The website displays the food truck user profile and, because it is a new account, prompts the user to enter details about the food truck's location, hours, and pictures.
  • John enters the food truck's current location, the hours, a few pictures, and hits submit on a button.
  • The website notifies the user that the profile is up-to date and returns to the food truck user profile home page where the new location is now displayed.
